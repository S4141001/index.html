Love is like the wild rose-briar, Friendship like the holly-tree—

But the holly is dark when rose-briar blooms, But which will bloom most constantly?

The wild rose-briar is sweet in spring, Its summer blossoms scent the air; Yet wait till winter comes again And who will call the wild-briar fair?

Then scorn the silly rose-wreath now And deck thee with the holly’s sheen,

That when December blights thy brow He still may leave thy garland green.,

--- THE END ---
